Stage details

STAGE 01

Proposal Accepted

Sales

The flow starts when a prospect signs off and the lead is marked Won in the Pipeline.

What happens automatically

  • Lead's status flip to won triggers a job-creation edge function.
  • A jobs row is created with the proposal's contract value, fence type, height, market, and PM assignment carried over.
  • Amiee gets a notification that a new contract is ready to set up.
Sales rep's job ends here. Once the lead is Won, all downstream billing is handled by Amiee + the PM + Virginia. The sales rep just confirms the proposal lines and signs off.

STAGE 02

Project Setup — Contract Tab

Amiee

Amiee opens the new job and confirms the financial baseline. This is the foundation every downstream step builds on.

Where to go

Open the job → Money group → Contract tab.

What to confirm

Net Contract Value
Carried over from the proposal — verify it matches the signed contract.
Sales Tax Amount
Auto-calculated from height/style. Verify against contract.
Bonds / Permit Amounts
Add if applicable.
Retainage %
Typically 5% or 10%. Confirms what the engine will withhold each cycle.
Billing Method
Progress (default) · Lump Sum · Milestone · T&M · AIA.

Verify before moving on

The Adjusted Contract Value at the top of the tab is what the system will measure billed % against. It must match the signed contract total (including bonds, permits, sales tax, approved COs).

Why this matters: if Adjusted Contract Value is wrong, every downstream over-bill check is wrong. A signed CO that isn't entered will trip Virginia's File Invoice block — even though the customer has agreed to the higher amount.

STAGE 03

Scope (Line Items)

Amiee

Line Items is the single source of truth Virginia's draft engine apportions PM bill sheets against. The labor / tax_basis split auto-derives — no separate Pricing Book to maintain.

Where to go

Same job → Money group → Scope tab.

Step by step

  1. Line Items are pre-seeded from the contract (qty / category / fence_type / height / unit price). Verify each row.
  2. The Labor / Unit and Tax Basis / Unit columns auto-fill from height and style. Read-only — the values are derived by the trg_jli_derive_split trigger:
    • Precast: tax_basis from height ($23 at 4ft → $31.75 at 10ft); labor = unit price − tax_basis.
    • Gates: labor = 66% of unit price; tax_basis = 34%.
    • Permits / Bonds: labor = 100% of price; tax_basis = 0 (non-taxable).
    • Stick-Built / Wood / Other: manual entry (left blank for now — engine emits a warning if the cell can't be billed).
  3. Click Save Lines. The engine reads from this table immediately on the Accounting tab.
Single source of truth. Line Items is the only place to maintain rates. Sales tax is calculated on the tax_basis portion only — the engine reads these columns directly when building Virginia's draft.

STAGE 04

Change Orders — Ongoing

Amiee

Throughout the life of the contract, scope changes require CO entry. This is the gap that historically caused over-billing.

Where to go

Same job → Scope tab → Change Orders card.

For each new CO

  1. Add the CO with description, amount, and customer signature reference.
  2. Add CO Line Items inside the CO card (same auto-derive applies — labor / tax_basis fill automatically based on category, height, style, and unit price).
  3. Mark the CO Approved when signed — only Amiee has this permission.
Critical: a CO entered without line items will inflate the Adjusted Contract Value but give the engine no rows to bill against. Virginia hits the over-bill block on legitimate billing. Always pair the CO header with line-item entry.
If you collapse a CO card while there are unsaved changes, you'll see a yellow warning banner inside the card. Click Save at the top of the EditPanel to commit, or expand the card to discard intentionally.

STAGE 05

PM Bill Sheet — Monthly

PM

Once a month per active job, the PM reports cumulative LF installed by stage. The PM workflow has not changed — same submission, same form, same fields.

Where to go

OPS app → PM Bill Sheet (mobile-friendly; tap-target sized for phones).

For each job in your queue

  1. Pick the job from your assigned list.
  2. Fill in cumulative LF since project start for each stage that has activity:
    • Precast: Posts Only · Posts & Panels · Complete
    • Stick-built: Foundation · Columns · Panels · Cleanup
  3. Enter your reported Invoiced Amount (total dollar value of work this cycle).
  4. Or click No Bill Required + reason (rain delays, awaiting permit, etc.).
  5. Submit.
Cumulative, not incremental. If you have 500 LF installed at Posts Only and 400 LF were already billed last cycle, report 500 — not 100. The engine subtracts prior billing automatically. This is the #1 source of cycle-to-cycle errors.

What happens after you submit

Your submission lands in the system and Virginia sees it within minutes on her Accounting queue. You don't get an email confirmation today — the PM Bill Sheet page shows your submission in history immediately.

STAGE 06

Review & File Invoice

Virginia

This is the new heart of the flow. Virginia reviews the auto-generated draft and files the invoice — replacing the Excel "Acct Sheet" template per job.

Where Virginia picks it up

  1. Open the job → Money group → Accounting tab.
  2. The Cycle Source dropdown auto-selects the most recent un-billed PM submission (no more hunting through the AR Pending list).
  3. The draft table populates automatically — the engine apportions the PM's per-stage LF across the Pricing Book proportionally to contract qty share.

What to review

  • Contract Summary card at the top — Contract / Billed / Pending / Balance / Retainage tiles + progress bar.
  • Double-Counting Banner — if the system spots a likely duplicate (legacy import + new draft, exact-amount match against a prior App, already over-billed), an amber banner appears with specific reasons + a collapsible Show Fix Steps guide.
  • Draft table — per-line × per-stage breakdown. Cells with red borders are over-billing warnings.
  • Single-stage rows (gates, options, permits, bonds) — Virginia toggles a checkbox or types a partial qty for these (the PM doesn't track them).

File the invoice

  1. Verify the totals.
  2. Click 📋 File Invoice ($amount).
  3. The system creates an invoice_applications record (App #N), posts to invoice_entries, and recalcs ytd_invoiced automatically.

What the system blocks

ProblemWhat Virginia sees
Already filedAmber "⚠ Already billed via Acct Sheet" pill in the Cycle Source dropdown
Likely duplicateAmber double-counting banner with reason + Show Fix Steps
Pending + Billed > ContractHard-block — File Invoice button disabled, reason shown
Approved CO neededGeneric "common causes" message in the Over-Bill Block modal

STAGE 07

Mark Paid

Virginia

When the customer pays, Virginia records the payment against the App.

Step by step

  1. In the App Ledger at the bottom of the Accounting tab, click ✓ Mark Paid on the relevant App row.
  2. Enter payment date, method, and reference (check #, ACH, etc.).
  3. Submit. The App row turns green; status flips to paid.
Partial payments are supported — the App stays filed until the cumulative payment matches the net due, then auto-flips to paid.

Drill into history

Click any App row to open the Drill-Down Modal:

  • PM Bill Sheet Source panel — shows the original PM submission: cycle, PM, submitted date, reported total vs filed amount (with drift % if any), per-stage LF table.
  • Line Breakdown — how the engine apportioned those PM totals to your pricing lines.
  • Payment History — all payments recorded against this App.

One modal, full chain, top to bottom — answer to "where did this number come from?"

STAGE 08

Release Retainage

Virginia

At job close, when retainage becomes due, Virginia releases it as a final invoice.

Step by step

  1. In the App Ledger, click ↳ Release Retainage.
  2. Confirm the held amount in the dialog.
  3. The system creates a final App, zeroes out the retainage_held balance, and posts to invoice_entries.
Release Retainage is header-only — no per-stage breakdown. The held amount is the full retainage withheld across all prior cycles, plus any released through Mark Final on individual COs (Phase E).

What changes for each role

Role Before Now
Amiee Set contract value + COs in Excel; no link to billing. Line Items in OPS feed Virginia's draft engine directly. Labor / tax_basis split auto-derives from height + style + unit price (no Pricing Book to maintain). CO setup is a forcing function — skip it and Virginia's draft fails.
PM Submit monthly bill sheet. No change. Same submission, same form, same fields.
Virginia Open Excel "Acct Sheet" template per job → manually plug PM numbers in → File invoice in QuickBooks. Open Accounting tab → review auto-generated draft → click File Invoice. App Ledger replaces the per-job Excel file. Drill-down modal replaces "where did this number come from?" digging.

Common questions

What if the PM enters the wrong LF on a stage?
Virginia spots it during draft review (over-billing warnings, drift % shown in the App drill-down). She can edit per-cell quantities via cycle overrides on the Accounting tab, or send the PM back to fix the bill sheet before filing.
What if Virginia files an invoice and the PM resubmits the same cycle?
The amber "⚠ Already billed via Acct Sheet" pill appears on the Cycle Source dropdown. The double-counting banner fires Rule C (exact-amount match) if the resubmission's total matches what was already filed. Virginia ignores the duplicate or works with the PM to file an amendment.
What if a job has historical billing from before the cutover?
Each historical invoice_entries row was synthesized into an invoice_applications row during the Phase A backfill. Those Apps carry a "Legacy Import" badge in the App Ledger; their dollar amounts are accurate but per-stage breakdown isn't available (the original Excel didn't capture it).
How does Amiee know if her CO setup is incomplete?
When Virginia opens the Accounting tab and the draft is wrong (e.g., over-billing because CO scope isn't priced), she pings Amiee. Phase E will add a Contract Readiness check that flags missing CO pricing before the next cycle starts.
What if the customer disputes the invoice?
Open the App row drill-down. The PM Bill Sheet Source panel shows exactly what the PM reported and when. The Line Breakdown shows how those totals became dollars. Forward the modal screenshot or quote the figures back to the customer with confidence.
Can Virginia still use the legacy AR Approve path on the Billing page?
Yes — for now both paths work. An amber 📋 Acct Sheet App #N pill appears on BillingPage rows when a submission has already been filed via the new Accounting tab — visual heads-up to prevent double-billing. Once Virginia is fully comfortable on the new tab, the legacy AR Approve path can be deprecated.
What does the Over-Bill Block modal show now?
When an approval would push the job past 120% billed, the modal pops with the red header + numbers as before. New: if double-counting is detected, an amber box at the top shows the specific reason(s) (e.g. "exact-amount match against App #5") plus a collapsible Show Fix Steps guide. If no double-counting is detected, the original generic "common causes" message remains. Override still requires typing the job number.
Where does the labor / tax_basis split come from?
It auto-derives from category + height + style + unit price the moment a Line Item is saved — no separate price book to maintain. Sales tax is calculated on the tax_basis portion only. Line Items is the single source of truth.
